Signal Graph

The figure below illustrates the signal graph including the adjusted parameters panel and measurement field.

The section 'Adjusted Parameters Panel' shows a summary of the adjusted vertical scaling, horizontal scaling, and trigger parameters. In this case, channel 1 and 2 was adjusted to 2.00V per division. If the label 'CH1:' or 'CH2:' contains a tilde symbol (~), the corresponding channel is displayed inverted. The horizontal scaling amounts to 1.00μs per division. Furthermore, the trigger parameters were set to rising edge on channel 1 for the voltage level of 1.00V. The trigger voltage level is indicated in the signal graph with a black line.

The 'Signal Graph' illustrates the currently measured signal. The 'Signal Graph' has 10 divisions on the x-axis and 8 divisions on the y which is similar to a traditional oscilloscope.

The 'Signal Graph' contains, in addition to the channel signals, a GND line for each channel signal and a trigger voltage level line. These lines can be disabled within the menubar under the section View. Just uncheck the corresponding line by clicking the desired menu item.

The lower bar of the 'Signal Graph' is called 'Measurement Panel' and shows the selected measurements. In this case the 'Measurement Panel' illustrates the parameters: Vpp, Vmin, Vmax, and f.

The dialog 'Channel Settings' (menu: Settings -> Channel) allows the user to invert the channel and to adjust the probe attenuation (10:1, 1:1). Furthermore, it is possible to select an individual signal color.
